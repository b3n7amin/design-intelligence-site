Five gaps internal evidence would resolve
Gap 01
Canada facility not yet activated
The world's first climate-neutral ceramic facility is the brand's strongest proof point. Internal activation strategy is invisible from the outside.
Gap 02
Designer partnerships under leadership transition
Whether interim CEO decisions are made through an efficiency lens touching the design programme — the brand's deepest structural moat — is not publicly visible.
Gap 03
Where V&B is winning — and why
The sales front line knows what buyers say when they choose V&B. That intelligence exists nowhere in the public record. It is the most commercially useful single input available.
Gap 04
Which Chinese competitors — precisely
Named brands, specific price points, which product categories entering first. Generic pressure is not actionable. Precision requires intelligence beyond public signals.
Gap 05
After-sales truth
What customers experience post-purchase. What repair crews see in the field. Where the quality promise is confirmed or undercut in use.
The strategic question both paths answer
What can the brand carry in a repositioning — and what will it cost to ask it to do something it is not built for?
This cannot be answered from public evidence. It requires primary research, internal data, and market intelligence. Both paths forward are designed to answer it.
The brand review told us where Duravit stands. The next phase tells us what it can do — and what it will cost to ask it to do something it is not built for.
02 Two engagement paths

The full engagement and the light engagement answer the same strategic question at different depths. The full path includes research across all five internal groups, external market intelligence, buyer interviews, and six voice registers. The light path is targeted — faster, lower investment, still actionable, designed to inform a Path A decision if the findings warrant it.

Research comes before voice work. The SensoWash register was built from public signals. Extending voice architecture to other product lines without internal research first risks building registers on incomplete foundations — particularly given the leadership transition and competitive pressure. Both paths include voice work. The difference is depth and number of registers.
Path A — Full Engagement
Complete Strategic Intelligence + Voice Architecture
Primary research across all five internal groups. Full external market intelligence. Buyer interviews. Complete diagnostic revision. Voice registers for four product lines plus internal and PR. Leadership-ready strategic brief delivered in person.
Internal interviews — 16–21 people across senior leadership (3–4, in-person preferred), product line managers (4–5), sales front line EU markets (4–5), after-sales and repair crews (3–4), brand and marketing team (2–3). 13–16 hours. Structured guides per group. AI-assisted transcription. Confidential synthesis.
Internal datasets — Sales conversion by tier and geography. After-sales and NPS data by product line. Designer partnership activity log. Half-day data briefing session format — no raw data export required.
External market intelligence — Statista and Euromonitor premium segment data. V&B Ideal Standard integration tracking. Precisely named Chinese competitors by category — specific brands and price points, not generic pressure. Specification community signals. Procured at cost, passed through.
Buyer interviews — 4–5 external buyers: premium renovation buyer (1–2), hospitality or project buyer (1–2), architect or specifier (1). Remote. Europe-first. Recruited via showroom contacts or specialist qualitative recruiter.
Voice Architecture — Governed registers for SensoWash (revision to production standard), ME by Starck, D-Neo, and one line confirmed by research findings. Plus internal communications register and PR and press register. Six documents total, each with language principles, tone calibration, worked examples, and vocabulary guide.
Strategic brief for leadership — What the research confirmed, what it changed, where V&B is structurally exploitable, where Chinese pressure is actually landing, what the brand can and cannot carry in a pivot, and three repositioning directions with brand implications. 03 Voice Architecture

A communications register governs how a brand sounds across every context — product copy, press, internal communications, campaign briefs, agency direction. Each register is derived from the diagnostic findings and primary research, not from style preference. The SensoWash register is the template.

04 What follows — immediate add-on and two further steps

One engagement can be commissioned immediately alongside either path. Two further engagements are available once the strategic brief is complete — for geographic extension and structural rebuild.

Immediate — available now, alongside either path
Brand–Leader Resonance Report · Immediate add-on
Michael Demmer — Brand to Leadership Alignment
The brand review named the leadership transition as the most time-sensitive structural risk in the entire diagnostic. Michael Demmer brings industrial transformation experience and operational discipline to a brand whose primary competitive advantage is design authority built over four decades. Those are not the same register. This report maps the structural alignment — and distance — between Demmer's character, communication style, and decision-making logic and the brand he is now leading. It identifies the specific dimensions where the mismatch creates active risk, and the governance conditions that would resolve it without requiring the incoming CEO to change who he is.
