The Considered Defector · EV9
Driven German premium for fifteen years. Now finds the badge signals conformity. Wants to defect but needs the alternative to be objectively, demonstrably better. Will not move on faith alone.
Ur-story — five stages
Stage 1 · Ordinary world
Someone whose taste has quietly moved past the objects they own. The gap between what they know to be good and what they're still driving has become impossible to ignore.
Stage 2 · The call
Something that doesn't try to be anything other than what it is. No performance. No announcement. Just the quality of a thing designed by people who knew exactly what they were doing.
Stage 3 · The threshold
The door closes with a weight that says nothing needs to be added. The interior doesn't show off — it holds. Every surface was chosen, not specified.
Stage 4 · Transformation
They drive in the quiet of something that has no gap between what it promises and what it delivers. That gap used to be normal. Now its absence is remarkable.
Stage 5 · Return
When asked, they explain their choice without defensiveness — with the calm of someone who has done the comparison and has nothing left to justify.

The Purpose-Led Flagship Buyer · EV9
The vehicle is a public statement of values. Wants the largest, most visible choice to be the most responsible one — without sacrificing presence or authority.
Ur-story — five stages
Stage 1 · Ordinary world
Someone for whom every significant decision is simultaneously personal and consequential. They don't separate who they are from what they choose. The vehicle is not exempt from this.
Stage 2 · The call
A car large enough to mean something — and honest enough to mean the right thing. Scale without apology. Presence without guilt.
Stage 3 · The threshold
They get in. Real space. Materials that speak of intention, not expenditure. Technology visible enough to be understood, invisible enough to trust.
Stage 4 · Transformation
They move through the world in something that makes no contradictions. For the first time, the size of the vehicle and the scale of their values occupy the same object.
Stage 5 · Return
Their family is in the back. The road ahead is open. Nothing has been traded away to get here.
