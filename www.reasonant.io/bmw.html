What this report is

A self-initiated diagnostic study

This report is a demonstration. It was not commissioned by BMW. It was produced to show what a structured diagnostic framework can find from public data alone — before a single internal interview is run, before any proprietary research is accessed, before a formal engagement begins.

The findings are not provisional. The structural reading of BMW's brand identity, the analysis of its live communications, the scoring of its product range and audience architecture — these conclusions hold on public evidence. What changes with primary access is not the direction of the findings. It is their precision, their confidence level, and their actionability.

This report is also an invitation. The engagement requirement blocks at the end of each section describe specifically what primary access — interviews, internal documents, customer research — would add to each finding. Together they constitute a brief for a full diagnostic engagement with BMW's brand and leadership team.

The intended reader

This report is written for Milan Nedeljković, Chairman of BMW AG, and for senior members of BMW's brand strategy leadership. It is addressed to the person most directly responsible for the brand's narrative trajectory over the next decade.

It does not tell him what BMW's products should look like or how to run the company. It reveals what BMW has always been at its structural root — and shows precisely where that truth is currently suppressed in how the brand communicates. The gap is real. The principle that closes it is already in the product.

How the diagnostic works

The framework behind this report tests internal brand coherence — whether a brand's emotional foundations, its live communications, and its leadership signals are pointing in the same direction. It begins with the brand's core drives and tests whether current output expresses or suppresses them.

This is not a market positioning study. It does not benchmark BMW against competitors on feature dimensions. It reads the brand as a structural system and identifies where that system is under stress — and why.

How to read the evidence in this report
Framework
Conclusions drawn directly from the structural diagnostic model. These findings are framework-validated — they follow from the brand's scored emotional architecture and the system's internal logic.

Each section ends with a confidence split — the proportion of findings that are framework-derived versus estimated. The lower the framework percentage, the more a section would change with primary access. The higher it is, the more the finding stands on structural evidence alone.

The diagnostic system behind this report was built to work from the inside out. This version works from the outside in. It finds what it finds. What it cannot yet see is named precisely — and that naming is the beginning of the conversation.

Introduction — how to read this report

This report examines the structural coherence of the BMW brand: whether what BMW says it is, what its communications perform, and what its incoming leadership signals are all pointing in the same direction. They are not, and that gap is what this report documents.

BMW was built on the deepest possible connection between a driver and a road. That principle is alive in every car. It is not yet alive in how BMW speaks — and a new leadership chapter has just begun.

The report moves through four layers: the market conditions that give the diagnosis urgency, the structural brand diagnostic with scored findings, the phenomenological depth — what the cars say, who the driver is, what life BMW offers — and the commercial consequence of closing or failing to close the gap. The sections build on each other. Each connects to the spine above.

This report is built entirely from public sources — campaigns, press releases, published interviews, and public market data. No internal BMW documents, customer research, or leadership access informed the findings. Where primary evidence is absent, that absence is named. Each section ends with a note on what primary engagement would add.

00.5

The principle BMW was founded on

The Communion Principle · internal name
The car that makes you more.

When a BMW is working as it was built to work, something happens that no other car in the category is engineered to produce. The driver and the machine become one system. The road speaks through the chassis to the body. The counter-forces, the grip at the limit, the weight shifting through a corner — all of this flows from road to driver without mediation, without flattery, without the dampening that comfort inserts between a person and what is actually happening. The driver does not use the car. The driver and the car are briefly, completely in communion.

The founding human story — 1959

In December 1959, BMW's board convened to vote on selling the company to Daimler-Benz. After ten hours of debate the vote was heading toward yes. One shareholder — Herbert Quandt, nearly blind from a retinal disease he had carried since childhood — had been quietly increasing his stake against all advice from his bankers. He changed his vote. He bet his fortune on a company making something he would never fully experience himself.

He believed in what the car was for — the precise connection between driver and road — before he could verify it in his hands. That conviction is the brand's first founding act. Public record

The founding engineer — 1962

Baron Alexander von Falkenhausen was asked to design an engine for the Neue Klasse with a 1.3-litre displacement. He refused. He convinced management that 1.5 litres was the minimum — with headroom to 2.0 — and fought the board over the five-bearing forged crankshaft they considered an extravagance. His bosses wanted three bearings. He built five. Not to be difficult. Because three bearings produced an engine that was less precisely responsive to the driver's input.

He refused the cheaper answer because the cheaper answer interrupted the communion. The M10 engine he built ran from 1962 to 1988. It powered Formula 1. He almost certainly never imagined that. That is the point. Public record

What communion produces
In that precise, unmediated response the driver finds out something about themselves. Not about the car — about their own capacity. An instinct confirmed. A limit found and then extended. The person who exits the car is more than the person who entered. Not transformed — expanded. And hungry for more road.
The tension — permanent and ownable
The car that makes you more vs the car that makes it easier. Every competitor is building toward easier — more isolation, more assistance, more protection from the full signal. BMW's founding position is the opposite: the car that needs you to be present, because only then does the communion work.
The principle in autonomous mode
When the car drives itself, the communion does not end — it changes channel. The expert passenger is immersed in the precision of what the machine is doing around them. Feeling the forces. Trusting the response. The interior becomes the new zone of communion. The principle survives the disappearance of the steering wheel.
The principle across markets
America — the longer road
The communion principle is native to American car culture — the road as self-discovery, the driver who becomes more capable through accumulated contact with a machine that tells the truth. The argument against Tesla is precise: technology communion makes you feel more advanced. Road communion makes you more capable. One expands what the machine can do. The other expands what you can do. Framework
China — 工夫 / gōngfu in motion
The communion principle has a Chinese cultural root that no competitor has claimed. Gōngfu — the deep skill acquired through dedicated practice that produces a state of perfect accord between practitioner and art. The tea master whose hands know the water temperature. The calligrapher whose brush responds to thought. BMW's communion is gōngfu in motion. The Neue Klasse interior, in autonomous mode, is the space for the expert to be present to mastery. Not the driver's gōngfu — the engineer's gōngfu, felt from inside. Framework Estimated
The principle is not new. Von Falkenhausen built it into the first engine in 1962. It is in the M10, in every generation of the 3 Series chassis, in the M cars, in the driving dynamics philosophy that BMW engineers have protected for six decades. It has never been named. It has never been claimed as the brand's governing logic. It has been expressed in metal and never in language. That is the gap this report documents — and that is the gap the Neue Klasse launch period is the right moment to close.

The context

The definition of luxury in automotive is undergoing a structural shift. This is the market condition that gives the brand diagnostic its urgency.

Old luxury model — pre-EV era
Mechanical moat
Premium buyers prioritised engine character, chassis feel, steering weight, craftsmanship, and engineering legacy. These are precisely the dimensions BMW owned most completely. The moat was mechanical, built over 90 years, and genuinely difficult to replicate. Public record
Emerging luxury model — EV era
Software moat
Younger premium buyers increasingly prioritise digital ecosystems, AI personalisation, seamless connectivity, and the feeling of being early to something that matters. EV platforms compress traditional differentiation — combustion character, drivetrain feel, NVH — are structurally diminished. Public record
China — the signal market
−15.5%
H1 2025 China sales
China is over 25% of global BMW volume. The standard framing is a software gap. The correct framing: BMW has a brand coherence gap in the market where incoherence costs you fastest. Public record
BMW is adapting
Not native

The mechanism
Coherence gap
A buyer who cannot feel the emotional case clearly will compare features — and lose to a brand that is coherent. The software gap is real. The brand gap is what makes the software gap commercially dangerous. Framework

02

The brand BMW actually is

BMW built its equity on a proposition that is unusually specific for a brand of its scale: the act of driving can be its own reward. Not transportation. Not status. The physical experience of a human being controlling a machine with precision and feeling the feedback of that control in the body.

"Freude am Fahren" — joy in driving — appeared in BMW advertising in 1936. It survived the war, the postwar recovery, the American market breakthrough, the SUV era, and the first wave of electrification. It survived because it was not a marketing proposition. It was a felt conviction.

The founding principle beneath that conviction is deeper than a slogan. BMW was built — from the first Neue Klasse engine — on the idea that a machine can be so precisely responsive to a human being that the two temporarily become one system. The driver feels the road through the car as if through their own skin. The car answers without mediation, without flattery, without the dampening that comfort inserts between a person and what is actually happening. And in that exact response the driver finds out something they did not know about themselves — a capacity confirmed, a limit extended, an instinct proven right. The person who exits the car is more capable, more confident, and hungrier for more road than when they got in. This is the communion principle. It has been expressed in metal for six decades. It has never been named.

Primary drive — very high
Challenger force
BMW's deepest drive is the refusal to accept limitation. "The Ultimate Driving Machine" is not marketing copy — it is a boundary condition. Observable in product decisions, not just communications. The M series is its clearest expression. Framework
Co-primary drive — very high
Desire and magnetism
BMW produces objects you want before you can fully explain why. This is not styling — it is a structural feature of how the brand approaches product. The premium price is downstream of this desire architecture. Framework
Secondary — high
Forward drive
BMW is permanently oriented toward what comes next. The Neue Klasse is not a product pivot — it is the forward drive made physical. Framework
Structural texture — moderate-high
Heritage depth
BMW's history is authority, not nostalgia. It gives the challenger force something to push against. Without it, the claim would be hollow. Framework
Structurally specific — not absent
Communion, not warmth
Warmth, playfulness, and community energy are structurally low — and correctly so. BMW does not offer warmth. It offers something more specific and more lasting: the communion of perfect response, the intimacy of a machine that confirms your own capacity without flattering it. That is not warmth. It is a deeper form of connection that the brand has never correctly named. Naming it is the commercial opportunity. Framework

03

What the cars say

For most of BMW's history, design said the same thing the slogan said. Proportions were driver-focused: long bonnet over the front axle, short overhangs, weight balanced between driver and road. The design was not decorative. It was a statement about what the car was for.

Phase 1 — Bangle era, early 2000s
First break: louder, still arguing
Flame surfacing divided opinion but was still making an argument: this car has energy contained in its body, motion implied at rest. The forward drive was intact. Controversial — not incoherent. Public record
Phase 2 — Grille era, 2019–2024
Second break: dominance over identity
Quieter and more damaging. BMWs grew heavier in visual mass and began leading with a grille sized to project authority rather than express identity. The 4 Series, 7 Series, and XM began to look like luxury products. That is not the same as looking like driving machines. Public record
Phase 3 — Neue Klasse, 2025–
Correction: form returns to driver
Van Hooydonk's philosophy: simplicity, longevity, calm. "The world is getting louder and louder — I'm happy if we bring a little peace and quiet." Smaller grille, cleaner surface language, tighter proportions, a form that says the experience is inside, not on the surface. Public record
The interior argument — Neue Klasse

The Panoramic iDrive keeps driver-relevant information clean and close — speed, navigation, charge — without covering the windscreen in data. The physical driving interface is not eliminated: van Hooydonk drew an explicit line: "The car is not completely switchless." Public record

The steering wheel retains haptic control surfaces. The central computer — named the Heart of Joy — manages power delivery and chassis response at a precision and speed no combustion system can match. Public record

The critical gap
Design solved it. Communications haven't.
The Neue Klasse is not saying: we added a screen and a battery. It is saying: the precision of the driving experience has been elevated. That argument is legible in the cars. It is not yet legible in the brand's communications. Framework Estimated
Heart of Joy — the communion system
A computer named for joy, managing the communion between driver and road at a precision that exceeds what the driver can consciously process. In driven mode: the system that makes the exact response possible. In autonomous mode: the system that becomes the communion itself — the technology that lets the interior passenger feel what the machine is doing and trust it completely. This argument has not been made. The name is the brief. Framework

What the copy is currently doing

BMW's live brand communications are running a pattern visible across multiple output types and consistent enough to be structural rather than incidental. The brand is leading with obligation and trailing with identity.

The brand that offers the deepest connection between a person and a machine — a communion that extends the driver's capacity and leaves them permanently more — is currently leading with the least personal things it makes.

The most revealing phrase in BMW's recent public language: From the iX3 launch press release, September 2025 — "It stands for electric mobility without range anxiety and for a digital experience that uses intelligent solutions to put our customers at the centre. Moreover, it stands for sustainable production committed to responsible action. But most of all, it starts a new chapter for driving pleasure." That "but most of all" is a rescue move. The brand lists three obligations, then attempts to recover its identity at the end of the queue. Public record

Pattern 1 — iX3 launch copy
Obligation first, identity last
The structure of the sentence performs exactly what is wrong: the pleasure claim arrives last, and defensively, as if the brand needed permission to say what it actually is. Public record Framework

Heritage as hedge
Brands reach backward for nostalgia when they cannot yet make the forward argument on its own terms. "Driving pleasure is timeless" is a softer claim than "driving pleasure in an electric car feels like this." BMW is making the softer claim. Public record Framework
Pattern 3 — visual system
Warmth in wrong register
The dominant imagery mode in 2024–2025 output is heritage and warmth: classic cars returning home, families sharing moments. Action photography expressing forward drive is present in M-series material only — not the dominant register. Public record Framework

Who BMW attracts

BMW's buyer base has a documented demographic centre — median age 56 in the US, household incomes well above category average, predominantly professional. But the demographic description misses the more important psychographic one.

The person who buys a BMW shares a specific orientation toward the driving experience that cuts across age, income, and geography. They experience the car as an instrument — something that extends their capability and responds to their intention. They notice when a car does not respond precisely.

The life BMW offers

Red Bull offers a specific life: the life of a person who lives at their own limit. Everything Red Bull does is a rendering of that one experience. BMW has an equivalent offer — more specific, more intimate, and more permanently transformative. It has never been correctly named.

The life BMW offers is the life of someone who has been in communion with a machine that responds exactly — and who carries what that communion produced. Not faster. More. The driver who has accumulated enough BMW miles has learned to trust their instincts because a machine has been honest enough to confirm them, precisely, without flattery, road after road. That trust does not stay in the car. It travels with the person. They take the other road. They ask more of the next corner. They make decisions they would not have made before the machine confirmed their capacity. The communion produces a person who is hungrier for more — more road, more experience, more of what they find out about themselves when the machine answers exactly.

In autonomous mode, the communion changes channel but does not end. The expert passenger is immersed in the precision of what the machine is doing around them — the forces, the weight, the engineering performing at a level they can fully perceive and trust. The interior is no longer a cabin. It is an instrument. And the person who inhabits it fully — who brings the sensitivity of a practitioner rather than the passivity of a passenger — exits with the same expanded sense of capacity.

The new chairman

Milan Nedeljković assumed the chairmanship of BMW AG on 14 May 2026. His appointment is universally described as a bet on operational execution: the right person to deliver the Neue Klasse platform at scale, manage the factory network through electrification, and hold the line on cost discipline. That analysis is correct — and it misses the most important thing about his arrival.

He is not the wrong person for the brand's emotional story. He is the only person currently at BMW who is saying, in operational language, the thing the brand needs to say in human language. His conviction about exact response — systems that respond precisely as required, flexibility as discipline, precision as a governing value — is not adjacent to the communion principle. It is the communion principle stated from the engineering side. He has been describing the brand's soul in the language of manufacturing. He does not yet know that.

What needs to happen

Three moves, in sequence. The sequence matters — the second does not work without the first, and the third does not work without the second. The window is the Neue Klasse launch period. The opportunity is the first 18–24 months of the new chairmanship — a moment when strategic direction is most open to definition.

1
Name the communion principle — publicly, from the founding story
The first move is not a campaign brief. It is a naming act. BMW has never publicly claimed the principle it has built every car to produce: the precise, unmediated communion between driver and road that makes the driver more capable, more confident, and hungrier for more. Von Falkenhausen built it into the M10 engine in 1962. It survived every transition since. It is in the Heart of Joy. It is in the chassis tuning philosophy that BMW engineers have protected for six decades. It needs one sentence that names it — and that sentence needs to come from the chairman who is already saying it in operational language. Nedeljković's conviction that systems must respond exactly as required is the founding condition of the communion. He is already speaking the principle. It needs to be translated from factory language to human language. That translation is this move. Framework
2
Restore the natural amplitude — and extend it to autonomous mode
BMW's current communications are running two levels below the natural expression range for its identity. The warmth and heritage work is competent but performing the wrong register. Action imagery — the road, the driver, the communion at speed — needs to return as the dominant register, not M-series-only material. Copy that leads with the communion claim, not rescues it at the end of an obligation list. And critically: the Neue Klasse interior needs to be communicated as the new communion zone — the space where the expert passenger is fully present to precision in motion. Not a cabin. An instrument. The principle in autonomous mode needs a communication form as specific and as confident as the principle in driven mode. Framework Estimated
3
Adapt the communion for America and China — same principle, different cultural root
The communion principle is not a European idea. In America it is the road as self-discovery — the driver who becomes more capable through accumulated contact with a machine that tells the truth. The argument against Tesla is precise: technology communion makes you feel more advanced. Road communion makes you more capable. In China the communion principle has an ancient root: gōngfu — the deep skill that produces perfect accord between practitioner and art. The tea master. The calligrapher. BMW's communion is gōngfu in motion. The Neue Klasse interior, in autonomous mode, is the space for the expert to be present to mastery. These are not translations of European values. They are discoveries that the principle is already present in each culture's deepest vocabulary. Both require locally co-created execution. Neither requires a different principle. Framework Estimated

Commercial outlook

BMW enters 2026 structurally healthier than most European automotive peers. It preserved profitability better than Volkswagen and Mercedes. It maintained manufacturing flexibility by not over-committing to pure-BEV too early. The Neue Klasse architecture is on track. This operational credibility is a precondition for brand credibility — a brand making a claim about precision that its products cannot support is incoherent. BMW's products can support the claim. The communications are not making it.

Methodology and transparency

This study uses a structural diagnostic approach that tests internal brand coherence — whether a brand's emotional architecture, its live communications, and its leadership signals are all pointing in the same direction. The diagnostic begins with the brand's emotional foundations and tests whether current output expresses or suppresses those foundations.

This report is built entirely from publicly available sources. No internal BMW documents, customer research data, or leadership access informed the findings. All sections carry a confidence split between framework-derived conclusions (drawn directly from the structural diagnostic model) and estimated conclusions (drawn from inference, public data extrapolation, or pattern matching without primary evidence).

Sections should be read as a layered diagnosis: the framework-derived findings are structural and stable; the estimated findings are directionally reliable but should be updated with primary research before being used as the basis for strategic decisions. Each section identifies specifically what primary access would change.
